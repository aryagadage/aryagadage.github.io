Papers

  1. Nonparametric analysis of Random Utility Models: Computational tools for Statistical Testing
    Very interesting paper - column generation method for the Euclidean distance calculations. It starts with a limited number of variables, and identifies new ones as needed through a heuristic pricing problem. They have a Github repo on the same - I still do not completely understand how they compute the V(O). my notes

    I am currently auditing Game Theory Models of Operation at Columbia University. We are currently dicussing the Cournot, Bertrand and "The Tragedy of the Commons" problem. This course is taught by Dr. Jay Sethuraman. my notes

    SIG's Doug Costa explains the 2008 Financial Crisis in a mathematical context. I have notes on the lecture and supplementary topics like the Gaussian Copula model, the correlation smile, etc. my notes
